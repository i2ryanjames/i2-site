The Challenge

Why Muslims?

Muslims represent the largest unreached peoples on the planet. Over 37,000 die without the gospel every day. Not only does Islam enslave over a billion who have never heard the gospel, more and more traditionally Christian nations are seeing a sharp increase in the number of Muslims living around them. Mosques are now becoming common even in European and American cities.

Islam is the biggest challenge for the church today — both on the mission field and in your local neighborhood. It is also the fastest growing challenge. Islam is the fastest growing religion in the United States. In 1907, there were 230 million Muslims in the world. By 2010, there were 1.6 billion. Islam doubled in twenty years. According to Pew Research, there are close to 1.8 billion Muslims as of 2015.

While Islam is the biggest challenge, only one percent of all Christian missionaries go to do direct ministry among Muslims. That's one missionary for every 420,000 Muslims — equivalent to having about five churches and 150 pastors for all of North America.

The Barrier

Why people don't reach Muslims

Most people are concerned or frightened by the growth of Islam and terrorism, but the church is called to have love and compassion for Muslims. Our primary response should not be fearful or political, but based on deep love and compassion to share the love of Jesus with every Muslim.

Most people do not reach Muslims because of fear. That fear takes three primary forms:

1

Fear of suffering. There is a strong fear of violence and suffering when most people consider engaging Muslims.

2

Fear of the questions. Muslims are typically trained and prepared to ask very aggressive questions. Most believers do not feel confident enough to answer them.

3

Fear of the unknown. Most believers do not feel like they understand Islam enough to challenge it directly and are afraid to engage.

Though the issue seems to be Islam, what these fears really expose is that many of us are not truly discipled. True disciples love Jesus wholeheartedly. Because they love Him above all, they do not fear what any person can do to them — they refuse to fear Muslims. Instead, they are a faithful witness of the gospel in all the world.

Islam itself is not the actual reason why we do not reach Muslims — lack of discipleship is. The ultimate solution to mobilizing the church to reach Muslims is to disciple people to follow Jesus.

The Imperative

Why the Church must be trained

Muslims represent the greatest challenge to the Christian church today, yet few are equipped to engage Muslims with the gospel. While the church has been reluctant, Muslims themselves have been quite active in their own form of evangelism known as "dawah."

One source says 200,000 students in Pakistan alone, ages 8 to 30, graduate from ultra-conservative Islamic religious schools every year with 8 years of training, 8 hours a day, and the ability to easily challenge most believers on the authenticity of the Bible. Each month, Saudi Arabia alone spends more on spreading Islam than the International Mission Board (IMB) spends in a year.

Because Islam both aggressively confronts Christianity and holds doctrines that directly challenge the most essential tenets of the Christian faith, it is imperative that the entire church is trained to be a faithful witness of the gospel to Muslims. Every believer must have a basic understanding of how to confront and evangelize Muslims because of Jesus' deep love for them.

i2 has the vision, resources, and ability to make a significant impact in equipping more Christians to effectively evangelize Muslims. We are uniquely positioned to become the change agent for the global body of Christ to fulfill her call to the Great Commission among Muslims.

The greatest problem in the world is not radical Islam — it is nominal Christianity. The answer to global Islam is a discipled and trained Church that loves Jesus!
— Joshua Lingel, President of i2 Ministries
